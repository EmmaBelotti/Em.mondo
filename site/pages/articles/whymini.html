🎭Why miniature painting/making?🎭

...and why you should try it too!

while drawing and painting have lower entry barriers (literally a pencil and a surface), miniatures require tools, specific paints and materials. Still, i think everyone should try this hobby at least once, because for me it was an eye-opening experience!

Do i suck at art, or do i suck at 2D art?

pupil is eyemancer's best friend! Pupil doesn't know it tho...

i painted my first mini because i was forced to: i had a dnd game scheduled, and too many grey monsters, not ready for the upcoming encounter.
Before then, i didn't consider myself an "artsy" person, because my art-related projects were all failures: I couldn't draw, i couldn't blend colors together or even shade something...
... well fuck GUESS WHAT? the MEDIUM was the problem!
i enjoyed painting those first test models, and the result were decent enough to paint some more!

...but this isn't about me, why should YOU try miniature painting, even as a non-artist?

Your canvas is ready, or you could easily make one!

Clampion throws his carrot-weapon-thingy at his preys, shippo fetches it!

drawing is 🔥HARD🔥! I have two blenders instead of arms and know NOTHING about anatomy! Painting other people's work is not that fun either, makes me think i'm a toddler drawing with crayons...
With miniatures, you could buy or 3d-print assembled ones, but also make your owns in less than an afternoon! I made 2 guides where i explain how to, and the results are (imo) megacool!
also, while sculpting is very hard to master, it's a tactile medium! You can use all the object around you as 🌟free texture🌟

The third dimension is your friend!

entrapped in a lost temple, this demon lures its preys with the treasures they crave the most...

the main difference between painting a 2D dude and a 3D model is, you guessed it, the EXTRA DIMENSION!
it sounds like a joke, but there are so many ways to use it to your advantage: you could, for example:

  • use a torch in a dark room, to know where to paint the light reflections
  • use watered-down paints (washes) to stain the deepest recesses, to create contrast and dark shadows
  • use a big brush with dried paint to hit only the exposed edges
the canvas reacts to your brush strokes, and i find it megacool!

Storytellers and something more...

born by a sin, those creatures contain profecies not yet fulfilled...

i try to give a name to every monster i make, and a reason for them to exist in this world. Their stories come up naturally, and meeting them in a roleplaying campaign feels x10 times more interesting than fighting a bunch of goblins or human warriors...
but some of them have a deeper meening: when i felt sad or stressed, i always tried to work on one of those creatures. Painting them is so relaxing and therapeutic! Having a concrete "reward" after feeling hopeless for a while is such a dopamine boost. It's like entrapping all my negative energy on something so small and ugly, that could be defeated by a flick of my fingers. It's one of the best feelings in the world, and i can't recommend this hobby enough!
